Why Does Finance Matter?

Financial Inclusion Assessment Tools

As defined by the World Bank, financial inclusion is the accessibility, responsibility, and sustainability of affordable financial products and services that meet the needs of individuals and businesses (Financial Inclusion, 2018). The attention received by financial inclusion is due to its potential contribution to economic and financial development that could promote inclusive growth and income equality. The welfare of people can be addressed by financial inclusion as people who are cash-constrained can have help in managing their cash more efficiently (Morgan & Yoshino, 2017).

Financial inclusion is intersectional because the factors that drive or hold it back vary across countries. For a long time, the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) has been planning its route to become "one of the world's top five digital economies" (USAID, 2020), with a rapidly expanding digital market that includes more than 416 million internet users and 853 million mobile phone subscriptions. Part of the Master Plan on ASEAN Connectivity 2025 is to push for plans to increase digital integration of business and finance secotrs to increase inter-country transactional efficiency, and this study aims to provide evidence for opportunities that can be taken advantage of to give all countries in the region, whether high-, middle-, or low-income, an equitable chance to better ride the globalization wave.

The visualization tools in this application are designed to allow the exploration, comparison, and visualization of selected 2017 ASEAN data from the World Bank's Global Findex involving 4 outlined areas: the Region Brief, Financial Accounts, Barriers, and Financial Technology.
